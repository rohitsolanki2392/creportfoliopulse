6. SECURITY DEPOSIT
           (a) Concurrently with the execution of this Lease by Tenant, Tenant has deposited with Landlord the sum of $________________ to be held as a security deposit (the "Security Deposit") by Landlord, without liability for interest thereon, as security for the full and faithful performance by Tenant of each and every term, covenant, and condition of this Lease. The Security Deposit shall not be mortgaged, assigned, transferred to, or encumbered by Tenant without the written consent of Landlord, and any such act on the part of the Tenant shall be without force and effect and shall not be binding upon Landlord.
 
           (b) If any Rent or other charges required by this Lease shall become overdue and unpaid or if Landlord makes any payments on behalf of Tenant, or if Tenant fails to perform any of the terms and covenants of this Lease, Landlord may, at its option, and without prejudice to any other remedy Landlord may have on account thereof, appropriate and apply the Security Deposit or so much thereof, as may be necessary to compensate Landlord for the payment of the Rent or other sums due from Tenant, or for any loss, damage, or expense sustained by Landlord resulting from such default on the part of Tenant; and in such event Tenant shall immediately without demand restore the Security Deposit to the original sum.  If Tenant fully and faithfully complies with all of the terms, covenants, and conditions of this Lease, the Security Deposit shall be returned in full to Tenant without payment of interest or other increment for its use, within thirty (30) days following the later to occur of (i) the Expiration Date and (ii) surrender of the Premises by Tenant in compliance with the provisions of this Lease.
 
           (c) If any bankruptcy, insolvency, reorganization, or other creditor-debtor proceedings are instituted by or against Tenant, the Security Deposit shall be deemed to apply first to the payment of any Rent or other charges due Landlord for all periods prior to the institution of such proceedings and the balance, if any, of the Security Deposit may be retained by Landlord in partial liquidation of Landlord's damages.
 
           (d) No holder of a mortgage or deed of trust to which this Lease is subject or subordinate shall be responsible in connection with the Security Deposit, unless such mortgagee or deed of trust holder actually receives the Security Deposit.
 
           (e) If Landlord transfers its interest in the Premises, Landlord shall have the right to transfer the Security Deposit to the transferee thereof. In such event, upon the delivery by Landlord to Tenant of the transferee's written acknowledgment of its receipt of the Security Deposit, Landlord shall be deemed released by Tenant from all liability or obligation for the return of the Security Deposit, and Tenant agrees to look solely to such transferee for the return of the Security Deposit.
 
           (f) If Tenant is in default under this Lease more than two (2) times within any twelve (12) month period, irrespective of whether or not such default is cured, then, without limiting Landlord's other rights and remedies provided for in this Lease or at law or equity, the Security Deposit shall automatically be increased by an amount equal to the greater of:  (a) two (2) times the original Security Deposit, or (b) two (2) months' Rent, which shall be paid by Tenant to Landlord immediately upon demand.

8. TAXES AND OPERATING EXPENSES
           (a) Commencing with the calendar year ________ , Tenant agrees to pay to Landlord each year or fraction thereof during the term of this Lease, as Additional Rent, Tenant's Pro Rata Share (as defined herein) of the increase in the following expenses (collectively, the "Operating Expenses") over Operating Expenses for the Base Year (as defined herein):
 
                 (i) "Real Estate Taxes", which shall mean all real estate taxes and all valorem taxes, surcharges, special assessments and impositions, general and special, ordinary and extraordinary, foreseen and unforeseen, of any kind levied on the Center.  Real Estate Taxes also shall include:  any and all other taxes, levies, betterments, assessments and charges arising from the ownership, leasing, operating, use or occupancy of the Center or based upon rentals derived therefrom, which are or shall be imposed by national, state, municipal. quasi-governmental, or other authorities, including, but not limited to, any business improvement or development districts or special taxing districts; and any other tax, excise, fee, levy, charge or assessment, however described, that may in the future be levied or assessed as a substitute for or an addition to, in whole or in part, any tax, levy or assessment which would otherwise constitute Real Estate Taxes, whether or not now customary or in the contemplation of the parties on the date this Lease is executed.  Real Estate Taxes shall also include expenses of tax abatement or other proceedings contesting assessments or levies.
                 (ii) All expenses incurred by Landlord in connection with the operation, management, maintenance, and repairs or replacements in the Center, including but not limited to electricity (except as directly billed to tenants of the Center); utilities and utility taxes; water and sewer charges; all costs, charges and expenses incurred by Landlord in connection with any change in provider(s) of any utility (including, without limitation, maintenance, repair, installation and service costs associated therewith); insurance; repair or replacement, maintenance and cleaning of the Center and grounds, and supplies necessary therefor; uniforms and dry cleaning; cleaning of windows; snow removal; cleaning service and management contracts with independent contractors; general overhead and administrative expenses; management fees; benefits (including group life insurance), pension payments, payroll taxes, wages, license fees, and workmen's compensation insurance for employees, including, without limitation, property managers of Landlord engaged in the operation, maintenance, or repair of the Center.
 
Operating Expenses shall not include any of the following:   expenses for capital improvements to the Center (other than capital improvement required by law or intended to reduce Operating Expenses); expenses for painting, redecorating, or other work which Landlord performs for any tenant of the Center; depreciation of the Center; base ground rent; leasing commissions; legal expenses due to default or breach of any lease; salaries, wages, or other compensation paid to officers of Landlord; and net income, excess profits, or franchise taxes, or other taxes imposed on or measured by the income of Landlord from the operation of the Center.
 
           (b) The term "Base Year" shall be the calendar year _____.
 
           (c) The term "Comparison Year" shall mean each calendar year during the Term following the Base Year.  Landlord reserves the right from time to time to change the Comparison Year for calculating Operating Expenses so long as such change does not have a material adverse effect on Tenant.
           (d) Payments shall be made as follows:
                 (1) Prior to or during each Comparison Year, Landlord shall furnish Tenant with Landlord's estimate of the Operating Expenses (hereinafter referred to as "Estimated Operating Expenses") for such Comparison Year. On the first day of each month during such Comparison Year, Tenant shall pay to Landlord as Additional Rent, one-twelfth (1/12th) of Tenant's Pro Rata Share of the amount, if any, by which Estimated Operating Expenses exceeds Operating Expenses for the Base Year.  From time to time, Landlord may revise Estimated Operating Expenses in which event Tenant's monthly payment shall be adjusted accordingly.
                 (2) Following the end of each Comparison Year, Landlord shall prepare and deliver to Tenant a statement of the amount of Tenant's Pro Rata Share of the increase, if any, in the actual Operating Expenses for such Comparison Year, or fraction thereof, over the Base Year, and such amount shall be considered Additional Rent.  For the purposes of this Section 8, Tenant's pro rata share ("Pro Rata Share") shall be ______% (i.e., the ratio between _______ (square feet of rentable area in Premises) and _______ (square feet of rentable area in Center)).
                 (3) If Tenant's Pro Rata Share of actual increases in Operating Expenses is greater than the amount of the Estimated Operating Expenses paid by Tenant, Tenant shall pay to Landlord the deficiency within thirty (30) days after delivery of said statement. If Tenant's Pro Rata Share of actual increases in Operating Expenses is less than the amount of the Estimated Operating Expenses paid by Tenant to Landlord, then so long as Tenant is not in default under this Lease, Landlord shall credit the excess against future monthly installments of Estimated Operating Expenses; provided, however, that for the calendar year during which the Term ends, Landlord shall refund any such excess to Tenant.  For any Comparison Year that falls only partially within the Term, Tenant's obligation for increases in Operating Costs for such Comparison Year shall be prorated based upon the ratio of (a) the number of days within such Comparison Year falling within the Term to (b) 365; provided that in computing the amount payable for the period between the commencement of the final Comparison Year and the Expiration Date, increases in Operating Costs shall be calculated by comparing the Operating Expenses for the Base Year to the actual Operating Expenses for the immediately preceding full Comparison Year, and any required payment shall be made by Tenant on or before the Expiration Date.  Tenant's obligation to pay its Pro Rata Share of Operating Expenses and Landlord's obligation to reimburse Tenant for any excess of Estimated Operating Expenses over actual increases in Operating Expenses reflected on any such statement shall survive the expiration or earlier termination of the Term.
 
           (e) If Tenant delivers a written request, Landlord shall promptly provide Tenant with a written summary of the Operating Expenses incurred for the Base Year and the Comparison Year with respect to any statement Tenant has received from Landlord.
 
           (f) Nothing contained in this Section 8 shall be construed at any time to reduce the Rent payable hereunder or serve as a credit against the Base Monthly Rent, assessments, or any other charges due from Tenant to Landlord under the terms of this Lease.
 
           (g) Operating Expenses for each calendar year shall be those actually incurred, provided, however, that if the Center was not at least one hundred percent (100%) occupied during the entire calendar year, or if any occupant is paying separately for electricity or janitorial services furnished to its premises, the Operating Expenses shall be adjusted to project the Operating Expenses as if the Center were one hundred percent (100%) occupied and as if electricity and janitorial services furnished to such premises were included in Operating Expenses.

10. ALTERATIONS; FIXTURES
Tenant shall not make any alterations, improvements or additions (collectively, "Alterations") to the Premises, Center or Common Areas without obtaining the prior written consent of Landlord.  Tenant shall not paint, install wallpaper or window treatments, carpet, cut or drill into, or secure any fixture, apparatus or equipment of any kind to any part of the Premises without first obtaining the written consent of Landlord. All Alterations made by Tenant with the exception of wallpaper, window treatments and custom paint colors shall remain upon the Premises at the expiration or earlier termination of this Lease, and shall become the property of Landlord, unless Landlord delivers written notice to Tenant to remove the same, in which event Tenant shall remove such alterations, improvements, and additions and restore the Premises to the same order and condition in which it was on the Commencement Date.  Prior to the Expiration Date or earlier termination of this Lease, Tenant shall remove any wallpaper and window treatments, repair and restore the walls to a building standard color to be chosen by Landlord.  Should Tenant fail to do so, Landlord may do so and collect the cost thereof from Tenant as Additional Rent.
With the exception of items of personal property, trade fixtures, and movable furniture and equipment which are removable by Tenant as provided herein, all improvements and installations made by Tenant or Landlord shall become part of the realty and the sole property of Landlord (unless the Landlord gives written notice to Tenant to remove the same) and shall remain upon and be surrendered with the Premises at the expiration or other termination of this Lease.
All trade fixtures, movable furniture, and equipment installed by Tenant on the Premises shall remain the property of Tenant and shall be removed at the expiration or earlier termination of this Lease, provided (i) that Tenant shall not at such time be in default under this Lease, and further (ii) that in the event of such removal, having repaired the damage caused by such removal, Tenant shall promptly restore the Premises to its original order and condition excepting only normal deterioration and ordinary wear and tear. Any such trade fixture not removed at or prior to such expiration or termination shall be and become the property of Landlord.  Lighting fixtures, partitions, ceilings, plumbing and electrical fixtures, telephone, computer or intercom wiring, cabinetry, and heating and air-conditioning equipment, whether or not installed by Tenant, shall not be removable at the expiration or earlier termination of the Lease, and shall become the property of the Landlord.
Tenant shall not install any furniture or equipment in the Premises or make any alterations to the Premises which necessitate any change in the heating, air-conditioning, plumbing, electrical, water or sewer systems serving the Premises, without the prior written consent of Landlord.
Inspections or approvals of the improvements to the Premises or plans for construction thereof by Landlord and/or Landlord's employees, agents or inspecting engineers, shall not constitute a warranty or representation as to the technical sufficiency, adequacy or safety of the plans, structures, any of their component parts, or any other physical condition or feature pertaining to the improvements, it being acknowledged by Tenant that Landlord has made or directed such inspections and approvals solely as a landlord in determining and protecting the value of its property for internal purposes, and not as an expert in construction-related matters.
